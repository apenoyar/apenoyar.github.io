Hi! My name is Anna. I dedicated the first part of my career to service; in the Peace Corps, non-profits, and local government. Now, I'm taking the plunge, going back to school to learn to work at something else I'm passionate about... design. In the future, I hope to marry the two, using my design skills to help people.

About Me

As an only child, I spent much (maybe most?) of my time at home entertaining myself, often times taking over the family computer. In grade school, I played with Microsoft Paint for hours. In middle school, I spent time programming games onto my TI83+ calculator (ok, I exaggerated, it was really Cosmo personality quizzes.) In high school, I made our Nordic Ski Team (I’m from Vermont) a webpage where my teammates could look up race and practice schedules. It also played a midi version of “Ice, ice, baby” when it loaded. I’ve always been drawn to both art and technology.

In college, I studied Environmental Sciences. I learned about trees, at one point knowing the scientific names and common names for the 75 most common trees and shrubs in the Northeast (please don’t ask me to name any now), and about the complex interactions that happen within an ecosystem, and how diversity keeps it strong. While I don't work in Environmental Sciences now, I still love to spend my free time outside, exploring, and being a very amateur photographer.

I used my science-learning and to my parent’s mild horror, joined the Peace Corps! During my first year in El Salvador, I really only spoke the most basic Spanish. It was lonely, but thankfully on some Peace Corps packing list, someone suggested bringing Crayons. I had, and that was how I was first able to connect with the children in my community: through art! Despite not knowing the words for things ideas, intentions, we were able to communicate.

These days I spend my work life at a small public transit agency in Northern California, where I get to learn the mechanics of route design, and every now and then, I get to design the decals for a bus!
